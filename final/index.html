We've Heard it Before.

The clichés that surround depression are things everyone has heard before. It is so commonplace that it is often overlooked.

“Just try to be more positive!”

The things people tell you when they are trying to help you...

“Why are you so Anti-Social?”

...often end up creating a stigma towards the person. A sense of discomfort of one's self is created.

"Why are you so tired all the time?"

These clichés develop into daggers in the mind.

They turn into voices that tourment the depressed.

"Why can't I be normal?"

The constant nagging of voices. The inability to make them stop.

"It won't ever go away."

Is there ever a light at the end of the tunnel? A hand that saves us before we drown?

"Why can't I overcome this? Why does it keep coming back?"

It's a constant fight within yourself.

"Do not let what you cannot do interfere with what you can do. — John Wooden"

It's hard to understand, hard to bring up. Depression is not "cliché". Keep fighting, keep trying.
